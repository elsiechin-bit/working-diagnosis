      <dt>Acute care</dt>
      <dd>PEACE then LOVE - protection, early active exercise, compression, elevation; then loading and vascularisation</dd>
      <dt>NZ pathway</dt>
      <dd>ACC for injury - early physio referral</dd>
      <dt>Recovery</dt>
      <dd>Proprioceptive training (balance board, single-leg stance), peroneal strengthening</dd>
    </dl>
  </div>

    <p class="panic-tile__label">Refer when</p>
    <ul>

      <li>Chronic instability (20-40% incidence) failing 3 months optimised physio - consider Brostrom-Gould</li>
      <li>Recurrent sprains despite rehab</li>
      <li>Persistent pain &gt;6 weeks - rule out occult injury</li>

<div class="tier-2">
  <p class="tier-label">❍ Presentation, investigation, and management</p>
  <p>Ankle sprain is the most common sports injury, affecting the lateral ligament complex in 85% of cases. Injuries range from grade I (ligament stretch) to grade III (complete rupture). The ATFL is injured most frequently, followed by the CFL. Examination elicits tenderness over the affected ligaments, swelling, and sometimes bruising. Anterior drawer and inversion stress tests assess ligament laxity. Syndesmotic injuries follow external rotation injury - the syndesmotic squeeze test may be positive and these heal more slowly than lateral sprains.</p>

  <p>Ottawa Ankle Rules guide imaging: X-ray if bone tenderness at key bony landmarks or inability to weight-bear immediately and in clinic. MRI is reserved for syndesmotic injury, suspected osteochondral lesion, or chronic instability when surgical planning is being considered.</p>

  <p>Physiotherapy starting within days of injury is critical: proprioceptive training, peroneal muscle strengthening, and functional ankle bracing or taping. NSAIDs provide short-term analgesia. Most acute sprains heal within 2-6 weeks. Chronic instability (recurrent giving way) requires ongoing proprioceptive training and may benefit from functional bracing; the Brostrom-Gould surgical reconstruction is reserved for those failing 3 months of optimised physiotherapy.</p>
</div>

<div class="tier-3">
  <p class="tier-label">❍ Referral criteria</p>
  <p>Ottawa-positive ankle warrants X-ray, arrangeable in primary care. High ankle sprain (syndesmotic involvement) should be referred to orthopaedics - healing is slower and surgical repair may be needed. Chronic instability unresponsive to 3 months of dedicated physiotherapy warrants orthopaedic consultation to discuss surgical stabilisation. ACC covers acute injury management and physiotherapy for eligible patients.</p>
</div>

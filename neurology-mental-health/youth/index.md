<p>Youth mental health is a genuine crisis in NZ by any measure. Rates of anxiety, depression, self-harm, and eating disorders among young people have risen markedly over the past decade.<sup>1</sup> Waitlists for secondary CAMHS are long. General practice is, by default, a frontline service.</p>

<div class="tier-2">
  <p class="tier-label">❍ Consultation approach and screening</p>
  <p>Adolescent presentations are often indirect: school refusal, somatic complaints (headaches, abdominal pain), irritability rather than low mood, or a caregiver presenting on behalf of a young person who has not consented to be there. Seeing the young person alone - briefly, with clear confidentiality limits explained - is best practice and builds the therapeutic relationship that makes future help-seeking more likely.</p>
  <p>The PHQ-A (adolescent version) and GAD-7 are appropriate from age 12+. For self-harm, ask directly - the evidence is clear that asking does not increase risk and often provides relief.<sup>2</sup> Frame it matter-of-factly: "Some young people who feel this way hurt themselves - has that happened for you?" A positive response opens the clinical conversation; the question itself does not create the risk.</p>
  <p>Establish confidentiality boundaries clearly at the start: what is kept confidential, and what is not (immediate safety risk to self or others). Young people are more likely to disclose if they understand and trust this boundary.</p>
</div>

<div class="tier-2">
  <p class="tier-label">❍ Medication in adolescents and eating disorders</p>
  <p>Fluoxetine is the only SSRI with robust RCT evidence in adolescent depression (NNT approximately 4, NNH for suicidality approximately 143 - benefit outweighs risk for moderate-to-severe illness).<sup>3</sup> The FDA black-box warning on antidepressants and suicidality in under-25s reflects increased suicidal ideation (not completed suicide) in short-term trials; do not let it discourage appropriate prescribing in severe illness - but monitor closely in the first 4 weeks after initiation. Sertraline is an acceptable alternative when fluoxetine is not tolerated.</p>

    <p class="pillar-label">Lifestyle Medicine</p>
    <h1>Physical <em>activity</em>.</h1>
    <p class="topic-dek">The most evidence-dense intervention in general practice. Not sport — movement.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="topic-body">
    <p>The current NZ and WHO guidance for adults is 150–300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week, or 75–150 minutes of vigorous-intensity activity, plus muscle-strengthening on two or more days. These are minimums, not targets.</p>
    <p>Sedentary behaviour carries independent metabolic risk even in people who meet the activity guidelines — the evidence now supports breaking up sitting time as a distinct clinical goal, separate from structured exercise.</p>
    <p>For older adults, balance and falls-prevention training earns its own recommendation. The sit-to-stand test (five repetitions, timed) is a validated, zero-equipment mortality proxy worth using in routine review.</p>
  </div>

❍ Cervical Screening

NZ Cervical Screening Pathway Workshop

HPV primary screening from 1 September 2023. Consultation support only -- not for patient self-assessment.

What changed from 1 September 2023
Old programme (pre-2023)
Test type Cervical cytology (smear test)
Interval Every 3 years
Start age 20
Who collects Clinician only
Trigger Abnormal cell changes
Colposcopy if High-grade cytology
New programme (from Sept 2023)
Test type HPV primary test
Interval Every 5 years (if HPV not detected)
Start age 25
Who collects Clinician or self-collected vaginal swab
Trigger HPV detected (virus, not cells)
Colposcopy if HPV 16/18, or other HR + high-grade LBC
Pathway quick reference
HPV Result Triage LBC Action Next recall
Not detected Not required Routine recall 5 years
HPV 16 or 18 detected Not required* Colposcopy referral Per colposcopy protocol
Other high-risk HPV
(not 16/18)		 Normal / ASCUS / LSIL Repeat HPV in 12 months 12 months
HSIL / ASC-H / AGC Colposcopy referral Per colposcopy protocol
12-month repeat HPV: not detected Routine recall 5 years
12-month repeat HPV: any HPV detected Colposcopy referral Per colposcopy protocol

*LBC is performed on the same sample for HPV 16/18 positive results but does not change the colposcopy referral decision. Triage LBC is reflex -- performed on the same collected sample, not a repeat visit.

Self-collected swabs Patients can take their own vaginal swab for HPV testing -- this is a significant change that reduces barriers for people who find speculum exams difficult or distressing. If the self-test is HPV positive, the patient needs a clinician-collected sample for full genotyping and reflex triage LBC. The self-test result itself does not include genotyping, so the pathway (16/18 vs other HR) cannot be determined from a self-test positive alone.

Special circumstances -- check HealthPathways for local guidance
Immunocompromised
HIV, solid organ transplant, or on immunosuppressive therapy: annual HPV testing is generally recommended. Refer to NCSP guidance or HealthPathways Canterbury -- do not apply the standard 5-year interval.
Post hysterectomy
Total hysterectomy (cervix removed): no longer eligible for screening. Subtotal hysterectomy (cervix retained): continue screening as normal. Confirm which procedure if uncertain.
DES exposure in utero
Annual colposcopy is recommended regardless of HPV result. Refer to gynaecology.
Post-treatment surveillance
After treatment for CIN or cervical cancer, recall is managed under a separate NCSP surveillance schedule -- not the routine 5-year interval. Check HealthPathways or colposcopy discharge letter.
Age under 25
Not eligible for the national programme. Incidental HPV positivity and low-grade changes before age 25 are usually transient; speculum examination is not routinely recommended. If symptomatic, manage clinically.
Age over 69
The programme ends at 69. If not adequately screened, an exit screen may be appropriate. "Adequately screened" means two consecutive HPV-not-detected results in the past 10 years. If uncertain, check NCSP enrolment status.

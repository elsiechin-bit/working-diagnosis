<p>Plant-based diets (vegetarian or vegan) can be very healthy when well-planned. The key is ensuring adequate protein, B12, iron, calcium, and other nutrients that are less common in plant foods. This handout covers how to eat a nutritionally complete plant-based diet.</p>

<h2>Types of plant-based diets</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Vegetarian:</strong> No meat, poultry, or fish, but includes eggs and dairy products.</li>
  <li><strong>Vegan:</strong> No animal products at all - no meat, fish, poultry, eggs, dairy, or honey.</li>
  <li><strong>Flexitarian:</strong> Primarily plant-based but occasionally includes animal products. Often the easiest to manage nutritionally.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Nutritional challenges and how to address them</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Protein.</strong> Plant proteins are complete when you eat a variety (legumes, whole grains, nuts, seeds). Include legumes (lentils, chickpeas, beans) daily. Tofu, tempeh, and seitan are complete plant proteins. Aim for 50 - 65 grams protein daily.</li>
  <li><strong>B12 - critical for vegans.</strong> Vitamin B12 is not naturally available in plant foods. Vegans need fortified plant milks, fortified cereals, or B12 supplements (500 micrograms 2-3 times weekly or 2000 micrograms weekly). Without B12, nerve damage develops.</li>
  <li><strong>Iron.</strong> Plant-based iron is less well-absorbed. Eat iron-rich plants (leafy greens, legumes, fortified grains) with vitamin C (citrus, tomatoes, peppers) at the same meal to boost absorption. Avoid tea and coffee with meals.</li>
  <li><strong>Calcium.</strong> Without dairy, choose fortified plant milks, leafy greens (spinach, silverbeet, kale), tofu made with calcium, legumes, and nuts. Aim for 1000 - 1200 mg daily.</li>
  <li><strong>Omega-3 fats.</strong> Include ground flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts, or an algae-based omega-3 supplement for EPA and DHA (important for brain and heart health).</li>
  <li><strong>Vitamin D.</strong> Limited plant sources. Consider a supplement, especially if you have limited sun exposure or dark skin tone.</li>
  <li><strong>Zinc.</strong> Plant sources are less available. Include legumes, whole grains, nuts, and seeds daily.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Building balanced plant-based meals</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Include legumes daily:</strong> Lentils, chickpeas, beans - in soups, salads, curries, or as main dishes.</li>
  <li><strong>Eat whole grains:</strong> Brown rice, oats, quinoa, wholemeal bread - these provide protein, fibre, and B vitamins.</li>
  <li><strong>Add nuts and seeds:</strong> Almonds, walnuts, pumpkin seeds, chia seeds - snack or sprinkle on meals for protein, healthy fats, and minerals.</li>
  <li><strong>Include lots of vegetables:</strong> All vegetables are nutritious. Leafy greens are especially important for iron and calcium.</li>
  <li><strong>Use plant milks fortified with B12 and calcium.</strong> Soy and pea milks have good protein content.</li>
  <li><strong>Include tofu or tempeh regularly.</strong> These are complete proteins and versatile in cooking.</li>
  <li><strong>Do not rely on ultra-processed plant products.</strong> Plant-based meat substitutes can be high in sodium and additives. Whole foods are better.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Practical meal ideas</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Breakfast: Oats with berries, nuts, and plant milk (fortified with B12)</li>
  <li>Lunch: Lentil soup with wholemeal bread</li>
  <li>Snack: Hummus with vegetable sticks and almonds</li>
  <li>Dinner: Chickpea curry with brown rice and leafy greens</li>
</ul>

<h2>Supplements for vegans - minimum requirements</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>B12:</strong> Essential. Non-negotiable. Take 500 micrograms 2-3 times weekly or 2000 micrograms once weekly.</li>
  <li><strong>Vitamin D:</strong> Strongly recommended, especially if limited sun exposure or dark skin tone.</li>
  <li><strong>Omega-3 (EPA/DHA):</strong> Consider if you do not regularly eat fortified foods or ground flaxseeds.</li>
</ul>

<h2>When to see a dietitian</h2>

<ul>
  <li>When starting a vegan diet - to learn adequate B12 supplementation</li>
  <li>To ensure your plant-based diet is nutritionally complete</li>
  <li>If you are pregnant or planning pregnancy on a plant-based diet</li>
  <li>If you have anaemia or deficiency symptoms</li>
</ul>

<h2>Questions to ask your doctor or dietitian</h2>

<ul>
  <li>What B12 supplement should I take?</li>
  <li>Do I need other supplements?</li>
  <li>Are my iron and calcium levels adequate?</li>
  <li>Is my protein intake sufficient?</li>
  <li>How often should I have blood tests to check nutrient levels?</li>
</ul>

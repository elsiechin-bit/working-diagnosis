<p>Shift work disrupts your natural eating and sleep rhythms, making it harder to eat well and sleep deeply. With planning and strategy, you can maintain reasonable nutrition and health despite irregular hours.</p>

<h2>How shift work affects health</h2>

<p>Shift work disrupts circadian rhythms (your body's internal clock), leading to poor sleep quality, digestive problems, weight gain, and increased disease risk. Eating at unusual times and limited food availability compound the problem. The earlier your shift starts, the more challenging nutrition becomes.</p>

<h2>Eating strategies for different shift patterns</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Night shifts.</strong> Eat a substantial meal before your shift (high protein, complex carbohydrates). Eat light snacks during the shift rather than one large meal. Avoid heavy foods late in the shift. Avoid caffeine within 4 hours of intended sleep.</li>
  <li><strong>Early morning shifts.</strong> Eat a substantial breakfast before leaving home. Pack snacks and lunch. Eat your largest meal at lunch, not dinner (this aids sleep).</li>
  <li><strong>Rotating shifts.</strong> Maintain consistent mealtimes around your sleep schedule rather than clock time. Eat soon after waking and several hours before sleep, regardless of the time of day.</li>
</ul>

<h2>What to eat during shifts</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Plan ahead.</strong> Prepare meals and snacks at home before your shift. You will make healthier choices.</li>
  <li><strong>Bring substantial meals and snacks.</strong> Do not rely on vending machines or takeaway. Pack sandwiches, containers of leftovers, fruit, nuts, and yoghurt.</li>
  <li><strong>Choose protein and complex carbohydrates.</strong> These sustain energy better than sugar and refined carbohydrates. Include nuts, cheese, whole grain bread, legumes, and fruit.</li>
  <li><strong>Avoid ultra-processed foods and excess sugar.</strong> These cause energy crashes and weight gain. They worsen sleep too.</li>
  <li><strong>Drink water, not energy drinks or excessive coffee.</strong> Caffeine in soft drinks and energy drinks interferes with sleep. Limit caffeine after early afternoon.</li>
  <li><strong>Avoid alcohol before sleep.</strong> While alcohol might make you drowsy, it disrupts sleep quality.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Managing appetite and digestion during shift work</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Eating pattern matters more than timing.</strong> Rather than fighting your body clock, eat your largest meal when you feel most alert (usually afternoon or evening).</li>
  <li><strong>Eat smaller, more frequent meals.</strong> This prevents digestive discomfort and maintains stable energy.</li>
  <li><strong>Avoid eating immediately before sleep.</strong> Heavy meals within 2 - 3 hours of sleeping disrupt sleep quality.</li>
  <li><strong>Address indigestion.</strong> Shift workers are prone to reflux and indigestion. Identify triggers and avoid them.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Strategies for maintaining weight and health</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Regular activity.</strong> Exercise helps regulate appetite, sleep, and weight despite shift work. Aim for activity around your waking hours.</li>
  <li><strong>Sleep hygiene.</strong> Dark, cool bedroom, consistent sleep time relative to your shift, limiting screens before bed - these matter more than ever.</li>
  <li><strong>Vitamin D.</strong> Night shift workers get less sun exposure. Consider a vitamin D supplement, especially in winter.</li>
  <li><strong>Consistency within shifts.</strong> If you work rotating shifts, keep your eating and sleeping pattern consistent around your shift schedule, not the clock.</li>
  <li><strong>Monitor weight and mood.</strong> Shift work increases risk of weight gain and depression. Track how you are doing and seek help if problems develop.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Sample eating patterns for night shifts</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Before shift (evening, 5 - 6 pm):</strong> Substantial meal - protein, carbohydrates, vegetables</li>
  <li><strong>During shift (10 pm - 6 am):</strong> Light snacks: yoghurt, fruit, nuts, toast. Small meal around 2 am if working until morning.</li>
  <li><strong>After shift (7 am):</strong> Light breakfast, then sleep. Avoid large meals before sleep.</li>
</ul>

<h2>When to see your doctor</h2>

<ul>
  <li>If you are struggling with weight gain</li>
  <li>If you have persistent sleep problems</li>
  <li>If you develop digestive problems</li>
  <li>If you are feeling depressed or anxious</li>
  <li>To discuss whether shift work is affecting your health</li>
</ul>

<h2>Questions to ask your doctor</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Is my diet adequate despite shift work?</li>
  <li>Should I take a vitamin D supplement?</li>
  <li>What can I do to improve my sleep?</li>
  <li>Are there treatments for shift work-related health problems?</li>
  <li>Should I be concerned about my long-term health doing shift work?</li>
</ul>

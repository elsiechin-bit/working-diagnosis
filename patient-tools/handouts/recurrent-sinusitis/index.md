<p>Recurrent sinusitis (more than three infections per year) affects many people and reduces quality of life. While frustrating, most cases respond well to preventive measures, environmental control, and appropriate medicine. Some people benefit from specialist evaluation.</p>

<h2>What recurrent sinusitis is</h2>

<p>Sinusitis occurs when the sinuses (air spaces in your skull and face) become inflamed or infected. In recurrent sinusitis, you get multiple episodes of pain, congestion, and sometimes infection. This can follow a cold, be triggered by allergies or environmental irritants, or result from anatomical narrowing or polyps in the nose.</p>

<h2>Symptoms of a sinus flare</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Facial pain or pressure, especially around the cheeks and forehead</li>
  <li>Nasal congestion and difficulty breathing through the nose</li>
  <li>Post-nasal drip - mucus running down the back of your throat</li>
  <li>Cough (especially at night from post-nasal drip)</li>
  <li>Thick, often discoloured nasal discharge</li>
  <li>Reduced sense of smell or taste</li>
  <li>Headache</li>
  <li>Sometimes fever and malaise if infected</li>
</ul>

<h2>Preventing flares - the most important step</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Regular nasal saline irrigation.</strong> Daily or twice-daily rinses with saline solution help clear mucus, reduce inflammation, and prevent bacteria buildup. Neti pots or squeeze bottles work well. This is the single most helpful preventive measure.</li>
  <li><strong>Treat allergies aggressively.</strong> If your sinusitis is triggered by allergies, control allergies with antihistamines, nasal steroid sprays, or allergen avoidance. Uncontrolled allergies trigger recurrent infections.</li>
  <li><strong>Avoid irritants.</strong> Cigarette smoke, air pollution, and chemical irritants trigger flares. Avoid exposure and improve ventilation at home.</li>
  <li><strong>Stay hydrated.</strong> Drinking water helps thin secretions and prevent sinus blockage.</li>
  <li><strong>Humidify your environment.</strong> Using a humidifier, especially in winter, helps prevent sinus drying and blockage.</li>
  <li><strong>Address nasal obstruction.</strong> A deviated septum or polyps may require specialist evaluation. Sometimes surgery helps prevent recurrence.</li>
  <li><strong>Manage reflux if present.</strong> Acid reflux can irritate nasal passages and sinuses. Treating reflux helps some people.</li>
  <li><strong>Avoid upper respiratory tract infections.</strong> Hand hygiene, avoiding close contact with sick people, and maintaining immunity help prevent infections that trigger sinusitis.</li>
</ul>

<h2>When you have a flare</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Increase saline rinses.</strong> Do this 3-4 times daily during a flare to clear secretions and reduce infection.</li>
  <li><strong>Use nasal decongestants cautiously.</strong> Decongestants help temporarily but should not be used for more than 3 days (rebound congestion develops). Consider nasal steroid spray instead.</li>
  <li><strong>Pain relief.</strong> Paracetamol or ibuprofen manage facial pain.</li>
  <li><strong>When to use antibiotics.</strong> Antibiotics do not help viral sinusitis (most cases). Only use them if you have signs of bacterial infection (very thick purulent discharge, fever, severe symptoms lasting more than a week). When prescribed, take the full course.</li>
  <li><strong>Steam inhalation.</strong> Breathing steam helps clear congestion.</li>
</ul>

<h2>When to see your doctor</h2>

<ul>
  <li>If you have recurrent sinus infections (more than three per year)</li>
  <li>If symptoms are not improving with saline rinses and home measures</li>
  <li>If you need assessment for allergies</li>
  <li>If symptoms suggest complications (severe headache, vision changes, swelling around the eye)</li>
</ul>

<h2>When to see an ENT specialist</h2>

<ul>
  <li>If preventive measures and medical treatment do not control symptoms</li>
  <li>To assess for nasal polyps or anatomical obstruction</li>
  <li>If surgery might help (septoplasty or endoscopic sinus surgery)</li>
  <li>For specialist imaging or detailed assessment</li>
</ul>

<h2>Questions to ask your doctor</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Do I have allergies contributing to my sinusitis?</li>
  <li>Should I be doing saline irrigation? How often?</li>
  <li>Should I use a nasal steroid spray?</li>
  <li>When should I take antibiotics?</li>
  <li>Should I see an ENT specialist?</li>
  <li>Is there an anatomical problem that might require surgery?</li>
</ul>

<p>The Mediterranean eating pattern is one of the most researched and evidence-based approaches to healthy eating. It emphasises whole foods, healthy fats, vegetables, and fish while limiting processed foods. People who follow this pattern have lower rates of heart disease, stroke, and chronic disease.</p>

<h2>What the Mediterranean pattern includes</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Abundant vegetables and fruit.</strong> Aim for variety and colour - tomatoes, leafy greens, peppers, eggplant, berries. These are the foundation of meals.</li>
  <li><strong>Whole grains.</strong> Choose wholemeal bread, brown rice, oats, and legumes (lentils, chickpeas, beans). These provide fibre and sustained energy.</li>
  <li><strong>Olive oil as main fat.</strong> Use olive oil for cooking, dressing, and drizzling. It is rich in heart-protective compounds.</li>
  <li><strong>Nuts and seeds.</strong> Almonds, walnuts, sesame, and other nuts provide healthy fats, protein, and fibre. A small handful (30 grams) is a good portion.</li>
  <li><strong>Fish and seafood.</strong> Eat fish at least twice weekly. Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, anchovies) are high in omega-3 fats, which protect heart health.</li>
  <li><strong>Limited poultry and eggs.</strong> Include chicken and eggs in moderation as protein sources.</li>
  <li><strong>Limited red meat.</strong> Eat red meat infrequently (less than twice weekly if at all). When you do, choose lean cuts.</li>
  <li><strong>Moderate dairy.</strong> Include cheese and yoghurt in moderation. Choose lower-fat versions when possible.</li>
  <li><strong>Minimal processed foods.</strong> Avoid ultra-processed foods, sugary drinks, and refined carbohydrates.</li>
  <li><strong>Water as main beverage.</strong> Drink water throughout the day. Wine is optional and moderate (one glass with meals for women, one to two for men).</li>
</ul>

<h2>Practical eating patterns</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Build meals around vegetables.</strong> Make vegetables the largest portion of your plate. Add grains and protein around them.</li>
  <li><strong>Use olive oil generously.</strong> Drizzle on salads, dip bread, cook with it. It is calorie-dense but nutritious and heart-protective.</li>
  <li><strong>Include legumes frequently.</strong> Beans, lentils, and chickpeas provide protein, fibre, and are affordable. Use in salads, soups, and main dishes.</li>
  <li><strong>Eat fish twice weekly.</strong> Aim for fatty fish for omega-3 fats. Grilled, baked, or steamed preparation is healthier than fried.</li>
  <li><strong>Snack on nuts and fruit.</strong> These satisfy hunger without processed foods.</li>
  <li><strong>Choose wholegrain bread.</strong> Choose dense, seeded varieties over white bread.</li>
  <li><strong>Eat seasonally.</strong> Seasonal vegetables are fresher, more affordable, and encourage variety.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Practical tips for eating Mediterranean in New Zealand</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Buy vegetables and fruit in season - they are cheaper and fresher</li>
  <li>Choose extra virgin olive oil (darker in colour, more flavour and nutrients)</li>
  <li>Buy canned tomatoes when fresh are expensive - they retain nutrients well</li>
  <li>Use canned and frozen fish - they are convenient and nutritious (check for added sodium)</li>
  <li>Buy legumes dried in bulk - they are affordable and store well</li>
  <li>Shop local markets for fresh vegetables</li>
  <li>Make soups and stews using legumes and vegetables - they are inexpensive and filling</li>
</ul>

<h2>Common questions</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Is olive oil high in calories?</strong> Yes, but calories from olive oil are nutritious and satisfy hunger. Balance with activity level.</li>
  <li><strong>Is this diet expensive?</strong> It need not be. Seasonal vegetables, legumes, and canned fish are affordable. It is cheaper than processed convenience foods.</li>
  <li><strong>Can I eat pasta on this diet?</strong> Yes, but choose wholemeal pasta, keep portions moderate, and load it with vegetables and legumes rather than heavy sauces.</li>
  <li><strong>What about cheese and dairy?</strong> Include in moderation as part of meals rather than as the main dish. A small amount of cheese adds flavour.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Health benefits</h2>

<p>Strong evidence shows that following a Mediterranean pattern reduces risk of heart disease, stroke, type 2 diabetes, and cognitive decline. It supports healthy weight, lower inflammation, and better overall health.</p>

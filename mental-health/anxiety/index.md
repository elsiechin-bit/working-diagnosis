    <p class="panic-tile__label">Don't miss</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Medical mimics: hyperthyroidism, arrhythmia, stimulant or caffeine excess, drug withdrawal, phaeo</li>
      <li>Comorbid depression with active suicidality</li>
      <li>Severe avoidance preventing work, school, or self-care</li>
      <li>Acute panic with chest pain in someone with CV risk - rule out ACS first</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="panic-tile first-line">
    <p class="panic-tile__label">First-line</p>
    <dl>
      <dt>Tool</dt>
      <dd>GAD-7 (≥10 probable); pair with PHQ-9</dd>
      <dt>First-line</dt>
      <dd>CBT - strongest evidence, effects outlast pharmacotherapy. Access &amp; Choice / PMHA</dd>
      <dt>Medication</dt>
      <dd>SSRI or SNRI - start low, titrate slowly (anxiety more activation-sensitive than depression)</dd>
      <dt>Panic specifically</dt>
      <dd>10 min explaining the panic cycle settles many mild cases</dd>
    </dl>

    <p class="panic-tile__label">Refer when</p>

      <li>Severe agoraphobia or housebound</li>
      <li>Suspected comorbid bipolar or psychosis</li>

<p>
  Anxiety disorders are the most prevalent mental health conditions in New Zealand and globally.<sup>1</sup> GAD, panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, and health anxiety each present differently and respond to different approaches. Conflating them leads to under-treatment. The consultation task is accurate diagnosis and directing treatment at the right target.
</p>

<div class="tier-2">
  <p class="tier-label">❍ Assessment and diagnosis</p>
  <p>
    The GAD-7 performs well for GAD and as a general anxiety severity screen across disorders (threshold ≥10 for probable GAD).<sup>2</sup> Pair it with the PHQ-9 in most presentations since depression and anxiety co-occur frequently and each can mask the other. Document baseline scores for monitoring treatment response.
  </p>
  <p>
    Distinguish between disorder subtypes at assessment, as treatment differs:
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>GAD:</strong> persistent, generalised worry across multiple domains, difficult to control, present more days than not for at least 6 months</li>
    <li><strong>Panic disorder:</strong> recurrent unexpected panic attacks plus persistent concern about future attacks or behavioural change because of them</li>
    <li><strong>Social anxiety disorder:</strong> fear of scrutiny or humiliation in social situations, often misread as introversion or low confidence</li>
    <li><strong>Health anxiety:</strong> excessive worry about having or developing a serious illness, disproportionate to symptoms or medical findings</li>
  </ul>

<p>Youth mental health is a genuine crisis in NZ by any measure. Rates of anxiety, depression, self-harm, and eating disorders among young people have risen markedly over the past decade.<sup>1</sup> Waitlists for secondary CAMHS are long. General practice is, by default, a frontline service.</p>

<h2>Consultation approach</h2>

<p>Adolescent presentations are often indirect: school refusal, somatic complaints (headaches, abdominal pain), irritability rather than low mood, or a caregiver presenting on behalf of a young person who hasn't consented to be there. Seeing the young person alone -- briefly, with clear confidentiality limits explained -- is best practice and builds the therapeutic relationship that makes future help-seeking more likely.</p>

<h2>Screening</h2>

<p>The PHQ-A (adolescent version) and GAD-7 are appropriate from age 12+. For self-harm, ask directly -- the evidence is clear that asking does not increase risk and often provides relief.<sup>2</sup> Frame it matter-of-factly: "Some young people who feel this way hurt themselves -- has that happened for you?"</p>

<h2>Medication in adolescents</h2>

<p>Fluoxetine is the only SSRI with robust RCT evidence in adolescent depression (NNT approximately 4, NNH for suicidality approximately 143 -- benefit outweighs risk for moderate-to-severe illness).<sup>3</sup> The FDA black-box warning on antidepressants and suicidality in under-25s reflects increased suicidal ideation (not completed suicide) in short-term trials; do not let it discourage appropriate prescribing in severe illness -- but monitor closely in the first 4 weeks.</p>

<h2>Eating disorders</h2>

<p>For eating disorders, early referral to a specialist service is time-critical -- medical instability and low BMI carry significant mortality risk and warrant prompt paediatric assessment alongside psychiatric care.<sup>4</sup> Anorexia nervosa has the highest mortality of any psychiatric condition. Do not wait for the patient to be "sick enough."</p>

<h2>Referral pathways in NZ</h2>

<p>Youthline (0800 376 633), 1737, and the local PMHA youth stream are the primary pathways for community support. For acute risk, refer to the ED or contact the regional crisis team. CAMHS referral criteria vary by locality -- check your local HealthPathways for current thresholds.</p>

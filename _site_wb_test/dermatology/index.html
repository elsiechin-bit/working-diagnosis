Dermatology

Skin in general practice

Dermatology makes up roughly one in five GP consultations in New Zealand. Most of it is entirely manageable in primary care — if the pattern recognition is solid.

This section covers the conditions that walk through the door most often: the eczemas, the fungals, the acnes, the rosacea variants, and — most importantly — the skin cancers that can't afford to be missed. New Zealand has one of the highest melanoma incidence rates in the world; that shapes everything about how we approach pigmented lesions here.1

Each condition page follows the same structure: what to look for, what to do, when to refer, and what to hand the patient on the way out. The clinician aside on each page links to the relevant BPAC or HealthPathways resource.

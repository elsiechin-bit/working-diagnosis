Lifestyle Medicine

Physical activity.

The most evidence-dense intervention in general practice. Not sport — movement.

The current NZ and WHO guidance for adults is 150–300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week, or 75–150 minutes of vigorous-intensity activity, plus muscle-strengthening on two or more days. These are minimums, not targets.

Sedentary behaviour carries independent metabolic risk even in people who meet the activity guidelines — the evidence now supports breaking up sitting time as a distinct clinical goal, separate from structured exercise.

For older adults, balance and falls-prevention training earns its own recommendation. The sit-to-stand test (five repetitions, timed) is a validated, zero-equipment mortality proxy worth using in routine review.

Hypertension in the Over-80s

Managing blood pressure in frail older patients requires a different calculus. The 2026 BPAC update shifts several thresholds.

Key thresholds

Ambulatory blood pressure monitoring is now preferred over clinic readings for diagnosis in most patients. A single elevated clinic reading is insufficient to initiate treatment - the 2026 guidance is explicit on this.

In patients over 80, the treatment target is 140/90 mmHg rather than 130/80. The evidence for aggressive targets in this age group remains weak, and the falls risk is real.

Notes

Var 01 is the safest pick - the original Atkinson Hyperlegible was specifically designed for low-vision readability and letter differentiation (b/d, p/q, i/l). Var 02 uses the newer variable version (Atkinson Hyperlegible Next) which allows heavier weights - more visual punch on headings but the same accessibility credentials. Vars 04 and 05 bring serif warmth to the body text and are closest in spirit to your current site's editorial feel, if that matters. All five share the same mono labels (JetBrains Mono), so that distinctive detail carries through.

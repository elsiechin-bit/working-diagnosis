Educational reference only - not medical advice. Chinese herbal medicine carries specific safety considerations not always present with Western herbs:
  • Quality & adulteration: patent products imported from outside regulated supply chains have been found to contain undeclared pharmaceuticals (corticosteroids, NSAIDs, sildenafil, sibutramine, warfarin), heavy metals (lead, mercury, arsenic - especially "mineral" preparations), and pesticides.
  • Substitution & misidentification: historical contamination of Stephania tetrandra (Han Fang Ji) with Aristolochia fangchi caused an epidemic of nephropathy and urothelial cancer in the 1990s. Aristolochia spp. are now banned in most jurisdictions.
  • Toxic herbs in routine use: Aconite (Fu Zi), Ephedra (Ma Huang), and Polygonum multiflorum (He Shou Wu) are commonly used in classical formulas but require correct processing (paozhi) and carry serious risk if misused.
  • Drug interactions: well documented with warfarin, immunosuppressants, antiretrovirals, and chemotherapy. Always disclose herbal use to clinicians.

Avoid completely: Guan Mu Tong (Aristolochia manshuriensis), Guang Fang Ji (A. fangchi), Ma Dou Ling (A. debilis), Tian Xian Teng, Qing Mu Xiang, Xun Gu Feng.

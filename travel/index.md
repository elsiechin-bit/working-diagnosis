    <h1>Going somewhere?</h1>
    <p class="topic-dek">A working reference for pre-travel consults and returning traveller assessment — built for the NZ general practice consultation, not the airport clinic.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="topic-body">

    <h2>The pre-travel consult</h2>
    <p>Placeholder — framework for a 20-minute pre-travel consultation: destination risk stratification, timing of vaccines, chemoprophylaxis decisions, and the things patients never think to mention (altitude, diving, sexual health, insurance).</p>

    <h2>Vaccines for travel</h2>
    <p>Placeholder — destination-based vaccine summary (Hep A, Typhoid, Yellow Fever, Japanese Encephalitis, Rabies, Meningococcal, Cholera). Cross-link to the Vaccines pillar for the NZ schedule baseline.</p>

    <h2>Malaria prophylaxis</h2>
    <p>Placeholder — drug choice by region (atovaquone-proguanil, doxycycline, mefloquine), contraindications, and counselling on compliance. Note areas of chloroquine resistance.</p>

    <h2>Traveller's diarrhoea</h2>
    <p>Placeholder — risk stratification by destination, standby antibiotic prescribing rationale, oral rehydration, when to recommend azithromycin vs ciprofloxacin in the NZ context.</p>

    <h2>Altitude sickness</h2>
    <p>Placeholder — AMS, HACE, HACE recognition; acetazolamide prophylaxis indications and dosing; practical ascent advice for patients heading to Nepal, Peru, Bolivia.</p>

    <h2>Fit to fly</h2>
    <p>Placeholder — post-op, post-MI, pneumothorax, late pregnancy, DVT risk and LMWH bridging. What airlines actually ask GPs to certify.</p>

    <h2>The returning traveller</h2>
    <p>Placeholder — systematic approach: fever (malaria first, always), diarrhoea, skin lesions, eosinophilia. Incubation periods as a diagnostic tool. When to refer to infectious diseases.</p>

    <h2>Special populations</h2>
    <p>Placeholder — immunocompromised travellers (live vaccine contraindications), pregnancy, infants and young children, older travellers with polypharmacy.</p>

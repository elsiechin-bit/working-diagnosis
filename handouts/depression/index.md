<p>Depression is more than feeling sad or having a bad week. It is a medical condition that affects how you think, feel, and function - often for weeks or months at a time. It is very common: around one in six New Zealanders will experience depression at some point in their lives. It is not a sign of weakness, and it is not something you can simply "snap out of."</p>

<h2>What depression feels like</h2>

<p>Depression affects people differently, but common experiences include:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Low mood most of the time - feeling sad, empty, or hopeless</li>
  <li>Loss of interest or enjoyment in things you used to like</li>
  <li>Changes in sleep - either too much or too little</li>
  <li>Low energy and fatigue</li>
  <li>Difficulty concentrating or making decisions</li>
  <li>Appetite changes - eating significantly more or less than usual</li>
  <li>Feeling worthless or excessively guilty</li>
  <li>Moving or speaking more slowly than usual, or feeling agitated</li>
  <li>Thoughts that life is not worth living, or thoughts of death or suicide</li>
</ul>

<p>To be diagnosed with depression, most of these symptoms need to have been present most days for at least two weeks, and be affecting your ability to work, study, or maintain relationships.</p>

<h2>What actually helps</h2>

<p>The good news is that depression responds well to treatment. Most people improve significantly with the right support.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Talking therapy.</strong> Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) is one of the most effective treatments for depression and has long-lasting results. Your GP can refer you to a psychologist or counsellor. Subsidised therapy is available in NZ - ask your GP about what is funded in your region. Some free services are available through Primary Mental Health funding.</li>
  <li><strong>Antidepressants.</strong> Medicines work well for moderate-to-severe depression and are often used alongside therapy. The most commonly prescribed are SSRIs (such as sertraline, fluoxetine, or escitalopram). They usually take 2-4 weeks to start working and 4-8 weeks to show full effect. They are not addictive. Your doctor will start at a low dose and adjust. Do not stop them suddenly without talking to your GP.</li>
  <li><strong>Physical activity.</strong> Exercise has strong evidence as a treatment for depression - equivalent to antidepressants for mild-to-moderate depression. Even 30 minutes of brisk walking three times a week makes a measurable difference. It can be hard to motivate yourself when depressed, but any movement helps.</li>
  <li><strong>Social connection.</strong> Depression pulls people inward. Staying connected - even when it feels effortful - helps. Peer support groups can also be valuable.</li>
  <li><strong>Sleep.</strong> Depression and poor sleep make each other worse. Prioritising consistent sleep habits helps both.</li>
  <li><strong>Alcohol.</strong> Alcohol is a depressant. It may feel like it helps in the short term but worsens mood over time. Reducing alcohol is worth trying.</li>
</ul>

<h2>If you are having thoughts of suicide or self-harm</h2>

<p>Please reach out right now. You do not have to manage this alone.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>1737</strong> - free call or text, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Trained counsellors, available to anyone in NZ.</li>
  <li><strong>Lifeline NZ</strong> - 0800 543 354, available 24/7.</li>
  <li><strong>Emergency</strong> - call 111 or go to your nearest emergency department if you or someone else is in immediate danger.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Recovery takes time</h2>

<p>Most people with depression improve, but it can take time - weeks to months. There may be setbacks along the way. Stay in contact with your GP, even if you feel you are not making progress. Let them know if things get worse.</p>

<h2>Questions to ask your GP</h2>

<ul>
  <li>What treatment options are available to me?</li>
  <li>What talking therapy services are funded in my area?</li>
  <li>If I start an antidepressant, what should I expect and when will I know it is working?</li>
  <li>When should I come back for a review?</li>
</ul>
